5 reasons Hull businesses are losing customers without a website

Callum MacInnes · May 2026 · 5 min read
The short version If your Hull business doesn't have a website, you're invisible to the majority of people who search for what you do before picking up the phone. Your competitors have one. They're getting the calls. Here's exactly why — and what to do about it.

In this article

  1. Your competitors are showing up on Google and you're not
  2. People can't find your phone number or opening hours
  3. You look less credible than businesses that have one
  4. Word of mouth has a ceiling — a website works 24/7
  5. You're invisible to anyone searching on their phone

Hull is a city that runs on small businesses. Cleaners, plumbers, roofers, electricians, personal trainers, dog groomers — the kind of trades and services that keep the city moving. And for years, word of mouth was enough. People knew someone who knew someone, and the phone kept ringing.

That's changing. The first thing most people in Hull do when they need a service is pick up their phone and search Google. Not ask a neighbour. Not check Facebook. Search Google. And if you're not there, you don't exist.

Here are the five ways not having a website is costing Hull businesses real money.

Reason 01

Your competitors are showing up on Google — and you're not

Search "cleaner in Hull" or "electrician Hull" and within seconds you'll see a list of local businesses with websites, reviews, and contact details. If you're not on that list, those customers are calling someone else. Every single time.

Google prioritises businesses with websites. A proper site with local SEO — your location, your services, your contact details structured correctly — tells Google exactly who you are and where you operate. Without it, you're starting every race from the back of the grid.

The businesses at the top of those searches aren't necessarily better than you. They just have a website.

Reason 02

People can't find your phone number or opening hours

Even customers who already know about you will Google your business name to get your number or check when you're open. If nothing comes up — or worse, if outdated information comes up — you've lost them before they've even called.

A website is your permanent, always-accurate contact card. It doesn't get lost, it doesn't go out of date unless you let it, and it's findable at 11pm when someone's boiler has broken down and they need an emergency plumber in Hull tomorrow morning.

That late-night search alone can pay for a website many times over.

People who find your number on your own website are higher-intent leads than people who found you on a directory. They searched for you specifically — they're already sold on giving you a call.
Reason 03

You look less credible than businesses that have one

When someone is deciding between two local tradespeople or services and one has a clean, professional website and one doesn't — the one with the website wins. Almost every time. It's not about the quality of the work. It's about perceived professionalism.

In Hull's competitive local market, a website signals that you're serious, established, and not going anywhere. It shows you've invested in your business. For customers spending hundreds or thousands of pounds on a service, that matters enormously.

Think about the last time you hired a tradesperson. Did you check if they had a website? Chances are you did — or you at least Googled them to see what came up.

Reason 04

Word of mouth has a ceiling — a website works 24/7

Word of mouth is brilliant. It's warm, trusted, and it converts well. But it has a hard ceiling. You can only reach the people in your existing network — and their network. Once you've exhausted that, growth stalls.

A website removes that ceiling. It's working while you're asleep, on a job, or on holiday. Someone in Hessle who's never heard of you searches "roof repair Hull", finds your site, reads about your services, and calls you. That couldn't happen with word of mouth alone.

The two work together brilliantly — happy customers who leave Google reviews directly improve how your website ranks. But word of mouth alone will only ever take you so far.

Reason 05

You're invisible to anyone searching on their phone

The majority of local searches in Hull — and everywhere else in the UK — happen on mobile phones. Someone walking past a property that needs cleaning, driving past a house with a dodgy roof, or sitting on the sofa wondering if they should sort their garden this weekend. They pick up their phone and search.

Google's mobile search results heavily favour businesses with mobile-optimised websites. If you don't have one, or if your site loads slowly or looks broken on a phone, you might as well not be there at all.
